Spring Salad with Herb Dressing

Return to main

This salad uses tender baby greens and seasonal veggies. Simple enough to make every day, yet sophisticated enough to serve to guests. Pair with a versatile homemade dressing, which is cheaper, healthier, and more delicious than any store bought dressing.

Spring mix is very delicate. If you would like to store this salad for later, do not add the dressing directly to the serving bowl; rather, drizzle on individual portions and leave the rest of the salad dry to prevent wilting.

Ingredients:

    For the dressing:

    Makes about 3/4 cup

  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard, smooth or grainy
  • 2 Tbsp plus 1 tsp wine vinegar - white, red, or champagne
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp expeller-pressed flaxseed oil
  • 1 tsp finely chopped fresh herbs, such as parsley, tarragon, thyme, basil, dill, or oregano
  • 1 clove garlic, peeled and mashed, optional

    • For the salad:

  • 6 cups spring mix
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced radishes
  • 1/2 cucumber, thiny sliced
  • 1 carrot, peeled and shredded on a box grater
  • 1/2 cup cherry or grape tomatoes, whole or halved lengthwise
  • 2 Tbsp shelled sunflower seeds

Instructions:

  1. Add the vinaigrette ingredients into a jar with a tight sealing lid and shake until the dressing is creamy, about 1 minute. (If using garlic, let sit for at least 1 minute more to infuse flavor before serving.)

  2. Put the spring mix in a salad bowl and top with radishes, cucumbers, carrots, tomatoes, and sunflower seeds.

  3. If serving immediately, pour the dressing over the top and mix gently to combine. If planning to save some for later, only add dressing to individual portions.

Note: This salad is wonderfully easy to customize. Use your favorite chopped nuts or seeds, chopped or grated vegetables, herbs, a little cheese, chopped apples, berries, or dried fruit. You could also turn this into a meal by adding grilled chicken, steak, or fish.

You'll have enough dressing to make at least two salads. Save the extra in a jar in your fridge for up to 2 weeks. Let the oil come to room temperature, then shake it again well before using leftovers.
